Socks are the gift you give the man who has stopped asking for things.
He won't tell you he needs them. He'll wear the ones with the holes
until you can see his ankles through them. He'll put them on after a
shower, look at the holes, sigh, and put them on anyway.

This is a short list of seven kinds worth replacing them with. One for
work, one for the woodpile, one for the porch in November, one for the
trail. None of them are flashy. All of them are the kind of thing he'll
wear out, eventually, and ask you to find more of.

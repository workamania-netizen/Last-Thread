A short list of fourteen things, for the kind of father who already owns
too much stuff and doesn't want any more of it.

The rule for the list: everything on it is something he wouldn't buy
himself, but would quietly love if you did. No gadgets that need charging
twice a week. No clothes that need ironing. Nothing that says "Best Dad"
on it in a font.

A note on price. We've tried to keep most things on the list under a
hundred dollars. The point isn't to spend a lot. The point is to find the
one thing he'll pick up first on a Saturday morning, every Saturday
morning, for the next ten years.
